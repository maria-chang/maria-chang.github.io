Hi, my name is Maria.

I study artificial intelligence at Northwestern University and I'm a member of the Qualitative Reasoning Group. Before grad school, I studied Cognitive Science at UC Berkeley and worked in Cognitive Neuroscience labs in the SF Bay Area and at Children's Hospital Boston. I'm interested in basic AI research as well as useful applications of AI. I think that intelligent software can have a huge impact on the accessibility of quality education.

I'm married and have two dogs.

I like bicycling, swimming and, to a lesser extent, running. I love making cocktails and I sometimes take on small art projects, like this.

Research Interests

My recent research activities have focused on applied artificial intelligence. In broad terms, I'm interested in:

  • Intelligent systems
  • Knowledge representation and reasoning, especially qualitative reasoning, analogical reasoning and spatial reasoning
  • The role of AI in education
  • The role of AI in human computer interaction

For my dissertation, I am building a system that learns qualitative science knowledge by reading and visually interpreting multimodal instructional analogies.

My advisor is Ken Forbus and my research is supported by the Spatial Intelligence and Learning Center, a cross-institutional NSF science of learning center.
